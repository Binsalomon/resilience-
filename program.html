Academy for Social Transformation's Programs:




Micro-Enterprise Development

Provide comprehensive training in business skills, financial literacy, and market strategies, enabling participants to create value-added agricultural products and non-farm businesses.

Savings and Investment

Promote a culture of saving, where participants are guided to save for their own equipment and to reinvest their earnings into their businesses for long-term sustainability.

Agricultural Innovation

Modern, sustainable farming techniques will be introduced alongside the provision of essential agricultural inputs, such as high-quality seeds, organic fertilizers, and tools. These resources will help boost productivity, diversify crops, and secure food availability, while creating surpluses for market sales.

Mindfulness Practices

Address the psychological trauma resulting from years of conflict through ongoing mindfulness, mental health support, and community healing initiatives.

Women's Economic Empowerment

Prioritize the inclusion of women, encouraging their leadership and acknowledging their pivotal role in community development.

Youth Empowerment

Equip young people with vocational and entrepreneurial skills, preparing them to become the next generation of business leaders and skilled workers.

Impact

We aim to create sustainable change by empowering 50 women and youth during 12-month pilot program with a focus on skills development, mental well-being and economic resilience, AST is transforming communities one step at a time

Women Participants (30)

Age Range: 18 to 50 years
Background: Former residents of Internally Displaced
Persons (IDP) camps in Northern Uganda
Significance: These women embody resilience, carrying firsthand experience of
conflict and displacement. Their participation bridges past challenges with future
opportunities, bringing vital perspective to our micro-enterprise initiatives.

Youth Participants (20)

Gender Distribution: 10 girls and 10 boys
Age Criteria: Born around or after 2006
Background: Children of parents who lived in IDP camps
Significance: This group represents Northern Uganda's post-conflict generation.
They inherit a complex legacy but possess immense potential for
driving transformative change in their communities.

This pilot group forms the nucleus of our 5 micro-enterprises, each enterprise comprising 10 members. This structure allows for focused skill development, close-knit teamwork, and efficient resource allocation. As these initial micro-enterprises take root and flourish, they will serve as proof of concept and inspiration for future expansion of Resilience from Within.



We invite you to join us on this journey toward creating resilient, self-sustaining communities. Together, we can build a brighter future for Northern Uganda. Will you take the step to make this vision a reality?
